# Phishing

Phishing is when attackers trick people into giving away their passwords or personal information by pretending to be someone trustworthy.

## How it works

- Attacker sends a fake email or message that looks real
- They pretend to be from a bank, social media, or company
- They ask you to click a link or enter your password
- The link takes you to a fake website that looks real
- They steal your information when you enter it

## Common types

1. **Email phishing**: Fake emails asking to verify your account
2. **Spear phishing**: Targeted attacks on specific people
3. **Clone phishing**: Creating fake copies of legitimate emails
4. **Smishing**: Phishing through SMS text messages

## How to protect yourself

- Don't click links in emails from unknown senders
- Check the sender's email address carefully
- Never enter passwords on links from emails
- Look for spelling and grammar mistakes
- Hover over links to see the real URL before clicking
- Use two-factor authentication
